Aberdeen's Rich History

Explore the deep-rooted heritage and storied past of the Granite City.

Origins & Early Days

With over 8,000 years of history, Aberdeen is one of Scotland's oldest cities. It started with the Pictish settlers who left behind remnants of their homes, giving rise to the region's ancient roots. By the medieval era, Aberdeen had already become a major center for trade, shipbuilding, and fishing. It developed into a royal burgh with two distinct areas—Old Aberdeen and New Aberdeen. The city still holds on to its traditions, with its historic streets and turreted buildings telling the story of Aberdeen’s enduring legacy.

The Industrial Revolution & The Granite City

During the 18th and 19th centuries, Aberdeen underwent massive transformations. The city's grey granite became synonymous with its identity, earning the city the nickname "Granite City." Iconic structures like Marischal College and St. Machar’s Cathedral were built, and industrialization brought expansion. As the city grew, so did its role as a center for commerce and innovation, cementing Aberdeen’s place in Scotland’s modern era.

Aberdeen in the Modern Era

In the 20th and 21st centuries, Aberdeen earned its title as the "Oil Capital of Europe" with the discovery of North Sea oil in the 1960s. The city flourished, attracting global talent and becoming a key player in the oil and gas industry. Aberdeen continues to grow, blending its rich cultural history with modern advancements in business, education, and sustainability. With its thriving university and breathtaking coastal landscapes, Aberdeen remains a dynamic and influential city.
